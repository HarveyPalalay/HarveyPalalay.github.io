Project 7

Data Science classification
metrics in Sci-kit Learn

One major area of predictive modeling in data science is classification. Classification consists of trying to predict which class a particular sample from a population comes from. For example, if we are trying to predict if a particular patient will be re-hospitalized, the two possible classes are hospital (positive) and not-hospitalized (negative). The classification model then tries to predict if each patient will be hospitalized or not hospitalized. In other words, classification is simply trying to predict which bucket (predicted positive vs predicted negative) a particular sample from the population should be placed. In this project, I explored a few classifications metrics in Python's scikit-learn and wrote my own functions from scratch to understand the math behind a few of them.

Project 12

Predicting Heart Arrhythmias
using Neural Network

Detecting abnormal heart beats, also known as arrhythmias, is a crucial aspect of cardiovascular health monitoring. Abnormal heart beats refer to irregularities or disturbances in the normal rhythm of the heart's electrical activity. These irregularities can manifest as a faster or slower heart rate, skipped beats, or an irregular pattern of beats. Accurate detection of abnormal heart beats is essential as they can be indicative of underlying cardiac conditions or other health issues. Some arrhythmias, such as atrial fibrillation, can increase the risk of stroke or other complications if left undetected and untreated. Therefore, early detection and monitoring of abnormal heart beats play a vital role in preventing potential cardiovascular problems and improving patient outcomes.In this project, we will train 3 neural networks (dense neural network, CNN, and LSTM) to predict heart arrythmias using ECG signal (continuous electrical measurement of the heart).
